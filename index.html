Save The River Movement Bangladesh

  • Who are we

    Bangladesh is a riverine country with 700 rivers flowing through her. Her natural, biological, and socio-economic wellbeing are inseparable to the wellbeing of these rivers. Bangladesh is the largest and most populated delta in the world. The major part of Bangladesh lies in the Ganges-Brahmaputra-Meghna Delta (GBM Delta). Many rivers of Bangladesh are dying fast. Rivers are being polluted, encroached, and river flows are artificially impeded.

    Save The Rivers Movement, Bangladesh (STRMB) started its journey in 2005 to protect the rivers of Bangladesh and to promote climate justice. STRMB is an advocacy group and a think tank. STRMB works toward establishing climate justice and water security in Bangladesh by collaborating with local and international stakeholders.

  • What we do

    Education : Save The River Movement, Bangladesh, facilitates climate literacy among general Bangladeshis. We have a comprehensive education program for schools, colleges, and universities.

    Collaboration : We collaborate with the government to create awareness about climate justice. We also make critical climate justice recommendations to the government, and many of our recommendations have brought about visible changes in protecting the rivers of Bangladesh.

    Engagement : Community engagement is the cornerstone of Save The Rivers Movement Bangladesh. We engage the communities who are the critical stakeholders of climate justice. Save The Rivers Movement Bangladesh has chapters around the country that are working tirelessly to protect our rivers and promote climate justice.

    Strategic Alliance : We know national borders do not confine waterbodies and climate. To ensure water security and establish climate justice, we partner with local and international organizations that work for similar causes for knowledge sharing and building strategic alliances.
